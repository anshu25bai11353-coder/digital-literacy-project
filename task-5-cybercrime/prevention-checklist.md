## 10 Essential Safety Tips

### 1. Never Share OTP, CVV, or UPI PIN
No legitimate organization, bank, or government official will ever ask for your OTP, CVV, or UPI PIN. If anyone requests this information, it is a scam. Hang up immediately and report the incident.

### 2. Verify Links Before Clicking
- Hover over links to view the actual URL before clicking
- Check for spelling errors such as "amaz0n.com" instead of "amazon.com"
- Look for "https://" and the padlock icon, but be aware that fake sites can also have these
- Type official website addresses directly into your browser when in doubt

### 3. Use Strong and Unique Passwords
- Create passwords with a minimum of 12 characters
- Include a combination of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols
- Example format: `Anshu@2026!Vit`
- Use different passwords for different accounts
- Avoid using personal information such as name, birthdate, or phone number

### 4. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
- Activate 2FA for email, social media, banking, and UPI applications
- Use authenticator apps like Google Authenticator or SMS-based verification
- This provides an additional security layer even if your password is compromised

### 5. UPI and Financial Safety Tips
- Verify the recipient's name before entering your UPI PIN for any transaction
- Never scan QR codes from unknown or suspicious sources
- Avoid keeping large sums of money in accounts linked to UPI applications
- Use only official applications: Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM
- Set daily transaction limits on your UPI apps

### 6. Keep Devices Updated
- Regularly update your phone, laptop, and tablet operating systems
- Install security patches promptly when they become available
- Updates address known security vulnerabilities

### 7. Be Cautious of Urgent Messages
- Messages creating urgency or panic are common scam tactics
- Scammers use phrases like "act now" or "your account will be blocked"
- Take time to verify independently before taking any action

### 8. Verify Sender Details Carefully
- For emails, check the full email address, not just the display name
- For SMS and WhatsApp, be cautious of unknown numbers
- Official organizations use verified domains such as `@vitbhopal.ac.in`

### 9. Avoid Sharing Personal Information Online
- Never post phone numbers, addresses, Aadhaar, PAN, or bank details on public platforms
- Refrain from sharing live location
- Exercise caution when posting in public groups and forums

### 10. Think Before You Click
- Avoid clicking on pop-up advertisements or suspicious banners
- Do not download attachments from unknown senders
- Install applications only from official Play Store or App Store

## Red Flags to Recognize

| Red Flag | Implication |
|----------|-------------|
| "Urgent! Act now!" | Scam tactic designed to create panic |
| "Your account will be blocked" | Fake threat to prompt immediate action |
| "You won a prize" | Fraudulent offer to trick victims |
| Unknown sender requesting OTP | Confirmed fraud attempt |
| Link containing spelling errors | Fake website designed to steal information |
| Offers that seem too good to be true | Almost certainly a scam |

## Immediate Actions If Victimized

### Step 1: Contact Bank or Payment App
- Call your bank's customer helpline without delay
- Use the application to block your card or account
- Report the fraudulent transaction immediately

### Step 2: File Report on National Cyber Crime Portal
- Visit: **cybercrime.gov.in**
- Select "Report Cyber Crime"
- Submit complaint with transaction details
- Anonymous reporting option is available

### Step 3: Call Helpline 1930
- **1930** is the national cyber crime helpline
- Available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
- Toll-free service to assist with fraud cases

### Step 4: Preserve Evidence
- Take screenshots of fraudulent messages, emails, and transactions
- Save transaction IDs and reference numbers
- Maintain a record of all communications

### Step 5: Change Compromised Passwords
- Update passwords for all affected accounts
- Enable two-factor authentication if not already active
- Create strong, unique passwords
